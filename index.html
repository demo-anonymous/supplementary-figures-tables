Emotion-Level Alignment in Embedding Space

Figure 2. Scale-dependent alignment to emotion prototypes across happiness, anger, and sadness. We compare generated samples against both real-emotion and neutral reference groups to calibrate similarity scores. Happiness exhibits a clear monotonic trend, while anger shows weaker asymmetry and sadness requires multi-latent modulation to achieve consistent alignment, indicating emotion-dependent controllability.

Table 1. Human evaluation with 20 raters. SAE improves both emotion expression (EMOS) and naturalness (NMOS) over Global Steering and Random SAE, indicating perceptually meaningful improvements.
